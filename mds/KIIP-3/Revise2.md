머리가 — head (subject) — टाउको
아픕니다 — hurts / is painful — दुख्छ
그래서 — so / therefore — त्यसैले
약국에 — to the pharmacy — फार्मेसीमा
갑니다 — go — जान्छु

1. 영화관에서는 휴대 전화의 (전원)을 끄세요.
English: Please turn off the (power) of your mobile phone in the cinema.
Nepali: सिनेमा हलमा मोबाइल फोनको (पावर) बन्द गर्नुहोस्।
Word-by-word breakdown:

영화관에서는 — at the cinema (topic) — सिनेमा हलमा (चाहिँ)
휴대 전화의 — mobile phone's — मोबाइल फोनको
전원을 — power (object) — पावर / बिजुली स्विच
끄세요 — please turn off — बन्द गर्नुहोस्
Options:

#	Korean	English	Nepali	Correct?
①	전원	power (on/off)	पावर / बिजुली	✓
②	화면	screen	स्क्रिन	✗
③	액정	LCD / display panel	एलसिडी डिस्प्ले	✗
④	전등	electric lamp / light	बत्ती	✗
Why ① is correct: You "turn off" (끄다) the power, not the screen, LCD, or a lamp on a phone.

2. 10도가 넘는 (일교차) 때문에 감기 환자가 갑자기 많아졌다고 합니다.
English: They say that cold/flu patients have suddenly increased because of a (daily temperature range) exceeding 10 degrees.
Nepali: १० डिग्रीभन्दा बढीको (दैनिक तापक्रम भिन्नता) का कारण रुघाखोकीका बिरामीहरू अचानक बढेका छन् रे।
Word-by-word breakdown:

10도가 — 10 degrees (subject) — १० डिग्री
넘는 — exceeding / over — भन्दा बढी
일교차 — daily temperature range (difference between day & night temp) — दैनिक तापक्रम भिन्नता
때문에 — because of — का कारण
감기 환자가 — cold/flu patients (subject) — रुघाखोकीका बिरामी
갑자기 — suddenly — अचानक
많아졌다고 — have become many / have increased (reported) — बढेका छन् भनेर
합니다 — (they) say — भनिन्छ / भन्छन्
Options:

#	Korean	English	Nepali	Correct?
①	습도	humidity	आर्द्रता / चिस्यान	✗
②	호우	heavy rain / downpour	मुसलधारे वर्षा	✗
③	한파	cold wave	शीतलहर	✗
④	일교차	daily temperature range	दैनिक तापक्रम भिन्नता	✓
Why ④ is correct: "10도가 넘는" (exceeding 10 degrees) describes a difference in temperature — that's 일교차 (the gap between day and night temps), which causes colds.

3. Dialogue
가: 욕실에 무슨 문제 있어요?
나: 네. 하수구가 (막혀서) 물이 잘 내려가지 않네요.

A: Is there a problem in the bathroom?
B: Yes. The drain is (clogged), so the water doesn't go down well.

A: बाथरूममा के समस्या छ?
B: हो। ढल (बन्द भएकाले) पानी राम्ररी झर्दैन।
Word-by-word breakdown:

욕실에 — in the bathroom — बाथरूममा
무슨 — what (kind of) — कस्तो / के
문제 — problem — समस्या
있어요? — is there? — छ?
네 — yes — हो / अँ
하수구가 — drain / sewer (subject) — ढल / नाली
막혀서 — because (it's) clogged/blocked — बन्द/जाम भएकाले
물이 — water (subject) — पानी
잘 — well / properly — राम्ररी
내려가지 않네요 — doesn't go down — झर्दैन / तल जाँदैन
Options:

#	Korean	English	Nepali	Correct?
①	새서	because (it's) leaking	चुहिएकाले	✗
②	잠겨서	because (it's) locked / submerged	बन्द/लक भएकाले	✗
③	막혀서	because (it's) clogged / blocked	बन्द / जाम भएकाले	✓
④	깨져서	because (it's) broken / cracked	फुटेकाले	✗
Why ③ is correct: Water not going down a drain = the drain is clogged (막히다). Leaking, locked, or broken don't fit the cause.

작성해서 — by writing / filling out (and then) — तयार गरेर / लेखेर
제출하라고 — to submit (indirect command) — पेश गर्न
하셨어요 — said (honorific) — भन्नुभयो
Options:

5. 가: 정월 대보름에 보통 뭐 해요? 나: 보름달을 보며 소원을 (빌어요).
English:
A: What do you usually do on Jeongwol Daeboreum (the first full moon of the lunar year)?
B: We (make) a wish while looking at the full moon.
Nepali:
A: 정월 대보름 (चन्द्र वर्षको पहिलो पूर्णिमा) मा सामान्यतया के गर्नुहुन्छ?
B: पूर्णिमाको चन्द्रमा हेर्दै इच्छा (माग्छौं / प्रार्थना गर्छौं)।
Word-by-word breakdown:

정월 대보름에 — on Jeongwol Daeboreum (first full moon festival) — 정월 대보름मा (पहिलो पूर्णिमा पर्व)
보통 — usually — सामान्यतया
뭐 해요? — what do you do? — के गर्नुहुन्छ?
보름달을 — the full moon (object) — पूर्णिमाको चन्द्रमा
보며 — while looking at — हेर्दै
소원을 — a wish (object) — इच्छा / कामना
빌어요 — pray for / make (a wish) — माग्छु / प्रार्थना गर्छु
Options:

#	Korean	English	Nepali	Correct?
①	빌어요	to pray / make (a wish)	माग्नु / प्रार्थना गर्नु	✓
②	보내요	to send / spend (time)	पठाउनु / बिताउनु	✗
③	즐겨요	to enjoy	रमाइलो गर्नु	✗
④	지내요	to spend time / live / get along	बिताउनु / जिउनु	✗
Why ① is correct: 소원을 빌다 is a fixed expression meaning "to make a wish." It's the standard collocation with 소원.
Why others are wrong:

② 보내요 — used with time/letters (시간을 보내다, 편지를 보내다), not with 소원.
③ 즐겨요 — means "enjoy," but you don't "enjoy" a wish; you make one.
④ 지내요 — means "to spend time / live"; doesn't pair with 소원 grammatically.
6. 동생이 지난달부터 학업과 직장 생활을 (병행하고) 있어서 정신없이 바빠요.
English: My younger sibling has been (juggling / doing in parallel) studies and work life since last month, so they are crazy busy.
Nepali: मेरो भाइ/बहिनीले गत महिनादेखि पढाइ र जागिर जीवन (सँगसँगै गरिरहेका) हुनाले अत्यन्तै व्यस्त छन्।
Word-by-word breakdown:

동생이 — younger sibling (subject) — भाइ / बहिनी
지난달부터 — from last month — गत महिनादेखि
학업과 — studies/academics and — पढाइ र
직장 생활을 — work/office life (object) — जागिर जीवन
병행하고 있어서 — because (he/she) is doing in parallel — सँगसँगै गरिरहेकोले
정신없이 — without (one's) wits / hectically — हतार-हतार / अत्यन्तै
바빠요 — is busy — व्यस्त छ
Options:

#	Korean	English	Nepali	Correct?
①	병행하고	doing in parallel / simultaneously	सँगसँगै गर्दै	✓
②	정리하고	organizing / tidying up	मिलाएर / सर्ने गर्दै	✗
③	참여하고	participating	सहभागी हुँदै	✗
④	되풀이하고	repeating	दोहोऱ्याएर	✗
Why ① is correct: 병행하다 = to do two things side by side. Doing studies + job at the same time is a textbook example of 병행.
Why others are wrong:

② 정리하고 — means "organize/clean up"; you'd organize one thing, not do two together.
③ 참여하고 — "participate" — you participate in something (one event), it doesn't capture juggling two.
④ 되풀이하고 — "repeat" — implies doing the same thing again; doesn't fit two different activities.

가입했다 — joined / subscribed — दर्ता भएँ / सदस्यता लिएँ
Options:

#	Korean	English	Nepali	Correct?

④ 접속했다 — means "to log on / connect" (사이트에 접속하다); a one-time access, not subscription.

9. 냉장고가 고장이 나서 <u>수리하려고</u> 서비스 센터에 전화했어요.
English: The refrigerator broke down, so I called the service center to repair it.
Nepali: फ्रिज बिग्रेकोले मर्मत गर्नका लागि सर्भिस सेन्टरमा फोन गरें।
Word-by-word breakdown:

냉장고가 — refrigerator (subject) — फ्रिज
고장이 나서 — because (it) broke down — बिग्रेकोले
수리하려고 — in order to repair — मर्मत गर्नका लागि

6. 저는 아침에 일어나면 바로 세수를 해요.
English: When I wake up in the morning, I wash my face right away.
Nepali: म बिहान उठ्ने बित्तिकै मुख धुन्छु।
Word-by-word breakdown:

저는 — I (topic) — म
아침에 — in the morning — बिहान
일어나면 바로 — as soon as (I) wake up — उठ्ने बित्तिकै
세수를 — face-washing (object) — मुख धुने काम

Options (closest to "일어나면 바로" = as soon as one wakes up):

#	Korean	English	Nepali	Correct?
①	일어나면서	while waking up	उठ्दै	✗
②	일어나려면	if (one) wants to wake up	उठ्न चाहेमा	✗
③	일어나는데	(as one) wakes up but…	उठ्दा तर	✗
④	일어나자마자	as soon as (one) wakes up	उठ्ने बित्तिकै	✓
Why ④ is correct: -(으)면 바로 ("right after ~") and -자마자 ("as soon as ~") have nearly identical meaning — both express that one action follows immediately after another.
Why others are wrong:

① 일어나면서 — "while waking up" — implies simultaneity, not sequence.
② 일어나려면 — "if (you) want to wake up" — sets a condition, not timing.
③ 일어나는데 — "as one wakes up but…" — adds contrast, doesn't capture immediacy.

Word-by-word breakdown:

민수 씨는 — Min-su (topic) — मिनसु जी
성격이 — personality (subject) — स्वभाव

Options (closest to "활발하고" = lively + and):

#	Korean	English	Nepali	Correct?
①	활발해서	because (he's) lively	फूर्तिलो भएकोले	✗
②	활발하면	if (he's) lively	फूर्तिलो भएमा	✗
③	활발하다가	(was) lively, then…	फूर्तिलो भइरहेर	✗
④	활발한 데다가	on top of being lively (also)	फूर्तिलो हुनुका साथै	✓
Why ④ is correct: -고 here lists two stacked qualities ("lively + kind"). -(으)ㄴ 데다가 = "in addition to / on top of being ~" carries the same additive meaning.
Why others are wrong:

① 활발해서 — "because lively" — gives a reason, not addition.
② 활발하면 — "if lively" — conditional, doesn't add a trait.
③ 활발하다가 — "was lively, then changed" — implies a switch in state, totally different meaning.

Why ④ is correct: The blank must restate the meaning of 과유불급 (過猶不及) = "excess is just as bad as deficiency / too much is no better than too little." Option ④ is a textbook paraphrase of this idiom. The conclusion "스마트폰에 의존하지 않고 잘 사용해야 한다" ("don't depend, use wisely") confirms this — moderation is the message.

Why others are wrong:

① — "Doing it long → becoming skilled" — irrelevant; the topic is excess, not duration of practice.
② — "Do a lot so it doesn't lack" — opposite meaning; encourages excess, contradicting 과유불급.
③ — "Excess is better than non-deficiency" — also opposite of the idiom; nonsensical against the moderation theme.

3. 작문 (Composition): 에스엔에스(SNS)와 의사소통
(Title: SNS and Communication)

📋 Required Content

✍️ Model Composition (Korean)
에스엔에스(SNS)와 의사소통
요즘은 스마트폰이 발달하면서 사람들이 에스엔에스(SNS)로 의사소통을 많이 한다. 카카오톡, 페이스북, 인스타그램 같은 에스엔에스를 통해 우리는 친구나 가족과 빠르고 편리하게 연락을 주고받을 수 있게 되었다.

에스엔에스로 하는 의사소통은 여러 가지 장점이 있다. 첫째, 시간과 장소에 상관없이 언제 어디서나 메시지를 보낼 수 있어서 매우 편리하다. 둘째, 사진이나 동영상을 함께 보낼 수 있어서 자신의 일상을 쉽게 공유할 수 있다. 셋째, 멀리 떨어져 있는 가족이나 친구와도 자주 연락할 수 있어서 외로움을 덜 느끼게 된다. 또한 외국에 있는 사람과도 무료로 연락할 수 있어서 경제적이다.

그러나 에스엔에스로 하는 의사소통에는 문제점도 있다. 첫째, 글로만 대화를 하기 때문에 상대방의 표정이나 목소리를 알 수 없어서 오해가 생기기 쉽다. 둘째, 사람을 직접 만나서 대화하는 시간이 줄어들기 때문에 인간관계가 점점 멀어질 수 있다. 셋째, 에스엔에스에 너무 많이 의존하면 중독이 될 수도 있고, 개인 정보가 유출되는 위험도 있다.

따라서 바람직한 의사소통을 하기 위해서는 에스엔에스를 적절하게 사용해야 한다. 간단한 일이나 급한 연락은 에스엔에스를 통해 하고, 중요한 이야기나 진심을 전하고 싶을 때에는 직접 만나서 대화하는 것이 좋다. 또한 에스엔에스를 사용할 때에는 예의를 지키고, 개인 정보를 보호하려는 노력도 필요하다. 이처럼 에스엔에스를 지혜롭게 활용한다면 더 풍부하고 따뜻한 의사소통을 할 수 있을 것이다.

📖 English Translation
SNS and Communication
These days, as smartphones have developed, people communicate a lot through SNS. Through SNS such as KakaoTalk, Facebook, and Instagram, we have come to be able to exchange contact with friends and family quickly and conveniently.

Communication through SNS has several advantages. First, it is very convenient because you can send messages anytime, anywhere, regardless of time and place. Second, you can easily share your daily life because you can also send photos and videos together. Third, you can frequently contact family and friends who are far away, so you feel less lonely. In addition, you can contact people abroad for free, which is economical.

However, communication through SNS also has problems. First, because you only converse through text, you cannot know the other person's expressions or tone of voice, so misunderstandings can easily occur. Second, the time spent meeting and talking with people in person decreases, so human relationships can gradually grow distant. Third, if you depend too much on SNS, you can become addicted, and there is also the risk of personal information being leaked.

Therefore, in order to communicate desirably, we must use SNS appropriately. For simple matters or urgent contact, we should use SNS, but when we want to convey important stories or sincere feelings, it is better to meet in person and talk. Also, when using SNS, we need to maintain etiquette and make efforts to protect personal information. In this way, if we use SNS wisely, we will be able to have richer and warmer communication.

🌸 Cultural Notes
💡 동창회 (Alumni Association): A Korean tradition where people who graduated from the same school (high school, university) regularly meet to maintain friendship. Common to organize annual reunions.

💡 온라인 → 오프라인 ("On→Off" trend): A modern Korean social phenomenon where people first connect through Naver Cafés, KakaoTalk groups, or apps like 소모임 (somoim, "small gathering"), then organize 정모 (정기 모임 = regular in-person meeting) where they meet face to face.

🌍 Cultural Notes
💡 미세 먼지 (Fine Dust): A major air quality issue in Korea, especially during spring (봄철). Often caused by yellow dust from China (황사) plus local pollution. Korea has 5 levels: 좋음 (good), 보통 (moderate), 나쁨 (bad), 매우 나쁨 (very bad). Schools and outdoor events get cancelled when 매우 나쁨 is forecast.

💡 취업 상담실 (Job Counseling Centers): Many Korean cities have 고용센터 (Employment Centers) and 외국인지원센터 (Foreigner Support Centers) where foreign residents can get free job counseling, resume help, and interview practice. KIIP graduates often qualify for these services.
